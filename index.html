03 — What to Wear

Style Guide

No theme. No matching shirts. No cowgirl costumes. The only dress code is: look as hot as humanly possible. Bring your best fits and stay on trend.

Thursday

Daytime

Travel day — wear something comfortable but cute. You're walking into a house full of your girls so show up looking good. Leggings-and-oversized-tee energy is fine, just make it intentional.

Night

Dinner in East Nashville — casual but put together. A great pair of jeans and a going-out top, a cute midi dress, or a skirt and tank. Nothing too dressy, we're easing in.

Friday

Daytime

Pool and exploring — bring a swimsuit you actually love (there will be photos), a coverup that doubles as an outfit, and something cute for walking around East Nashville.

Night

Dinner at Virago then Broadway — this is your moment. A satin mini, a slip dress, a mesh top, whatever makes you feel like that girl. Heeled boots or strappy sandals. Go off.

Saturday

Daytime

Brunch out and activities — hot but comfortable. Linen sets, denim shorts done right, or a sundress. You will be photographed. Golden hour group photos are happening.

Night

Ordering in and rooftop bars — start cozy, end hot. Wear something you can lounge in that also works if we decide to go out. A great dress or a top-and-pants moment.
